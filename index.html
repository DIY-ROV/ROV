Our diy ROV

Welcome to Our DIY Underwater ROV Project!

We built a low-cost, functional underwater ROV for exploration and research. Designed for under $375, it features a wireless remote, a waterproof casing, and a camera for capturing underwater footage.

Key Features:

  • Affordable & Accessible – A budget-friendly alternative to commercial ROVs.
  • Custom Engineering – Efficient motor control, buoyancy adjustments, and durable waterproofing.
  • Easy to Operate – Wireless remote control for smooth navigation.

Exploration Challenges

Exploring shallow underwater environments poses significant challenges due to the high cost and complexity of traditional underwater drones. Our goal is to develop an affordable solution that retains key functionalities while making underwater exploration more accessible.

Cost Comparison

Traditional remotely operated vehicles (ROVs) cost between $3,000 and $140,000, limiting their accessibility to large organizations and research institutions. Our project focuses on designing and building a functional ROV for under $375, making it an ideal option for hobbyists, educators, and researchers on a budget.

Our Goal

Our mission is to create an innovative yet cost-effective DIY underwater rover. We aim to achieve a balance between affordability, ease of assembly, and functionality, ensuring the ROV is capable of capturing underwater footage, maneuvering efficiently, and withstanding underwater pressure.

Design Iterations

We refined our design through multiple iterations, incorporating improvements in size, weight, and efficiency. Key changes included optimizing the frame structure for better hydrodynamics, upgrading propulsion for improved maneuverability, and refining waterproofing techniques to prevent water damage. An example from our slideshow includes using 3D printing to develop lightweight yet durable components, reducing overall material costs while enhancing structural integrity.

Power & Coding

The ROV's power system was designed for efficiency. Using an Arduino microcontroller, we integrated pulse-width modulation (PWM) to achieve smooth and precise motor control. The 12V to 5V converter was implemented to efficiently distribute power to different components, ensuring optimal performance while maintaining battery efficiency.
